# CATalyst Max — The Roadmap: Hard Brainstorm

Every CAT roadmap on the internet tells you *what* to study. That's commodity — ChatGPT does
it for free. Max's roadmap has to do something nobody else does: **diagnose why YOU fail,
prescribe the mechanism that fixes it, schedule the exact questions, adapt every single day,
and make you believe — with real math — that your target is reachable.**

This doc is the brainstorm for that. Core seed insight (Sahil): coaching DPPs are *identical
for everyone* and *blind* — they can't know if a question is easy/hard *for you*, or that your
real problem isn't the concept but applying it. Max's roadmap is the opposite of that.

---

## 1. The core thesis: Structure is table-stakes. Diagnosis + Adaptation is the product.

Three layers, in order of how rare they are:
1. **Structure** (what to study, when) — every roadmap has it. Necessary, not differentiating.
2. **Diagnosis** (*why* you fail, specifically) — almost nobody does it. This is the edge.
3. **Adaptation** (the plan changes daily based on what you did) — nobody does it at all.

Max leads with 2 and 3. The aspirant feels: *"this isn't a generic plan — it understood my
exact problem, and it's adjusting to me like a person would."*

---

## 2. The diagnosis engine — naming the REAL problem

"Weak in Quant" is useless. The roadmap diagnoses along these axes (from interview + practice):

- **Concept gap vs Application gap.** The #1 hidden problem: *"I understand the concept but
  when I solve, I can't."* That's NOT a theory problem — it's pattern-recognition / "how to
  start." Re-teaching theory is wasted; the fix is graded application reps focused on *starting*
  the question. The roadmap must tell these apart and prescribe differently.
- **Speed vs Accuracy.** Accurate-but-slow ≠ fast-but-wrong. Opposite fixes. Never time someone
  whose accuracy isn't there yet ("speed comes after accuracy").
- **Failure mode per topic:** don't-know-how-to-start / run-out-of-time / panic-freeze /
  silly-mistakes. Each needs a different drill (start-drills / timed-laddering / untimed-calm /
  checking-discipline).
- **Trap susceptibility.** Some keep falling for the same trap type (Distortion, Extreme
  language, Out-of-scope in RC; sign errors, unit traps in Quant). Drill the trap, not the topic.
- **Prerequisite holes.** Failing Time-Speed-Distance because Ratios are shaky → fix the
  foundation first. The roadmap respects the dependency tree.
- **Confidence level.** A 3/10-confidence aspirant needs engineered early wins; an 8/10 needs
  strategy and stretch, not pep talks.

The diagnosis is shown back to them in plain words ("your Quant isn't a concept problem — it's
that you freeze on how to *start* a question. We fix that with X.") — and *that* recognition is
half the magic.

## 3. The DPP engine — the "100 things" that make a daily set intelligent

A coaching DPP = the same 15 questions for 500 students. A Max DPP is assembled per-person from
the question bank using these rules (the engine; invisible to the user):

1. **Difficulty laddering.** Start each topic at the level they last *succeeded* at. +1 on a
   streak of correct, −1 on a struggle. Never start above their proven level.
2. **Confidence-first sequencing.** Open every session with a question they'll likely get right
   (momentum), end with one stretch. Win → effort → win.
3. **Concept-vs-application routing.** If theory is known but accuracy low → application reps
   that drill *starting the question*, not theory.
4. **Mistake recall (spaced repetition).** Today's wrong micro-topic returns tomorrow, then
   day 3, day 7, day 15 — at widening intervals, until mastered. (Sahil's example, generalized.)
5. **Micro-topic targeting.** Not "Algebra" — "quadratic discriminant," "remainder cycles,"
   "para-jumble openers." Precision is the point.
6. **Interleaving.** Mix topics within a set so they learn to *recognize* question types — CAT
   never labels the topic, so practicing un-labeled is the skill.
7. **Trap drilling.** Repeated trap susceptibility → a targeted mini-set on that trap.
8. **Frequency weighting.** More questions from high-CAT-frequency topics (PYQ trend data) —
   max marks per hour.
9. **Forced balance.** Won't let them hide in their strong subject; ensures all three move.
10. **Fatigue-awareness.** Low-energy day (from daily check-in) → shorter, easier set to keep
    the chain alive ("One Thing" mode: 3–5 questions on bad days).
11. **Plateau detection.** Accuracy stalls on a topic → switch approach (new technique, a video,
    drop to an easier base) instead of grinding the same wall.
12. **Mock-driven reshuffle.** A logged mock reshapes the next week toward its weak spots.
13. **Adaptive volume.** More reps where they're *close to a breakthrough*, fewer where mastered.
14. **Diagnostic checkpoints.** Periodic mini-tests recalibrate difficulty + priority so the
    plan never drifts from reality.
15. **Set-based LRDI.** LRDI practiced as full timed sets (how CAT tests it), not loose questions.
16. **Fix Mode baked in.** Every wrong answer → logged → re-drilled until fixed (the existing loop).
17. **"Why this, today."** Every DPP carries a one-line reason ("Percentages — it cost you 4
    marks last mock and shows in 8/10 papers"). Reasoning = trust.
18. **Streak/burnout protection.** Built-in rest days, grace days, no guilt-loop streaks.
19. **Readiness gating.** Don't unlock a hard topic until its prerequisites are green.
20. **Return-after-gap handling.** Came back after disappearing → a gentle re-entry set, not a
    pile of overdue work.

(These are the engine. The user never sees them — they see "Today: 12 questions · ~40 min.")

## 4. "You can actually score your target" — the scope-of-improvement design

The roadmap must make them *believe*, with honest math, not motivation fluff:

- **The gap, made concrete.** "You're at ~70%ile. Your target 90%ile is about +N correct per
  section — that's ~4 questions a section. Four. Here's the path to those four." Turning an
  abstract dream into a countable, doable delta is the unlock.
- **A projected trajectory.** "At this pace: ~78%ile by mock 3, ~86%ile by mock 6, target by
  CAT — *if* you hold the plan." Shows a future they can see themselves in.
- **Honest band, not a promise.** "Realistic range for your start + time: 82–90%ile. Where you
  land inside it is effort, not luck." Honest hope beats false hope (which dies at the first bad
  mock).
- **Named first win.** "By week 2 you'll have closed Percentages — your single biggest leak."
  A concrete, near, believable win.
- **The leak audit.** Show where their marks are *bleeding* now and how much each costs — then
  the plan attacks the biggest leaks first (ROI ordering).

## 5. Two-layer structure: simple map + detailed GPS (Sahil's idea, refined)

The tension: deep adaptivity vs not-overwhelming. Resolve it by hiding depth under a simple
surface.

- **Long-term = the MAP (simple, motivating).** A few phases, one headline each:
  *Foundation → Build → Sharpen → Mock-Grind → Peak.* A glanceable arc with the CAT date. No
  150-day wall. This is the "I can see the whole journey and it's doable" view.
- **Monthly = the THEME.** "This month: lock Arithmetic + start LRDI sets." One paragraph.
- **Weekly = the GPS (detailed, the only fully-detailed view).** This week's daily DPPs, topics,
  videos, the mock, each with its "because." Only the current week is fully open; next week
  previewed, rest locked behind completing this one.
- **Daily = TODAY (zero decisions).** "Today: 12 Quant · 1 RC · ~40 min · 1 Fix session." One tap.

**Deep engine, simple surface.** All of §3 runs underneath; the aspirant only ever sees a calm
daily card and trusts it.

## 6. Why this is NOT a ChatGPT roadmap (the moat, said plainly)

ChatGPT gives generic advice once and forgets you. Max:
1. **Hands you the actual questions** — scheduled daily DPPs from a real CAT-PYQ-based bank.
   ChatGPT can't give you a curated daily set to solve.
2. **Adapts every day to your mistakes** — yesterday's errors shape today. ChatGPT has no memory
   of your practice and no question bank.
3. **Diagnoses your specific failure mode** (concept vs application, your traps) — ChatGPT gives
   surface advice; Max prescribes the mechanism.
4. **Is grounded in 10 years of CAT PYQ frequency** — real pattern intelligence, never prediction.
5. **Runs the Fix Mode loop** — every mistake logged, diagnosed, re-drilled.
6. **Shows real, tracked improvement** — graphs, trajectory, the leak audit closing over time.

ChatGPT gives a plan. Max *is* a system that runs your prep and adjusts to you. That gap is the
whole product.

## 7. The emotional arc of the roadmap reveal

In order, what they should feel:
*"it understood my exact problem"* → *"oh — my target is only 4 questions away"* → *"I can see
the path and the first win is close"* → *"it's telling me exactly what to do today, I don't have
to figure anything out"* → *"if I just follow this, I think I can actually do this."*

The DPP schedule is what makes it land: seeing *specific questions scheduled for them* turns a
plan into a system. That's the moment they think *"if I had these resources, this would change
everything"* — which is the ₹99 → ₹999 conversion.

## 8. What we build for the TEST (this week) vs the real engine (later)

The full adaptive DPP engine is weeks of work and gated behind validation. For the **test**, the
LLM generates a *convincing, personalized sample roadmap* from the interview profile:
- the scope-of-improvement projection (the gap math + honest band + trajectory),
- the simple phase map,
- a **detailed sample Week 1** with specific scheduled DPPs (topics, micro-topics, difficulty,
  counts, the "because" on each) + the adaptive promise ("each day adjusts to your mistakes"),
- the diagnosis, shown back in plain words.

This is enough to validate the *output* — does it make a real aspirant feel *"this is for me and
I can actually improve"*? If yes, we build the real engine. The sample even references the
question bank we're extracting now, tying the two tracks together.

**Test flow:** Interview → Synthesis → Sample Roadmap → ask them: *did the interview feel like
someone understood you? does the plan feel built for you? do you believe you could hit your
target with it? would you want the daily DPPs?*
